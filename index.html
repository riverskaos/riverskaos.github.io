A Day in the Life and Times of Melissa Ashley

"I have much to write to you, but I do not want to use paper and ink. Instead, I hope to visit you and talk with you face to face, so that our joy may be complete." 2 John 1:12

An Identity Crisis

   I find it pertinent to first define the word your eyes immediately were drawn to: crisis – ‘a time when a difficult or important decision must be made’. I know what you’re thinking. And the answer is yes, I am overdramatic most days, but if I weren’t then why would you be here reading what I seem to have no courage to say aloud? I found that upon arriving to college, I indeed found myself in an identity crisis. I had to decide how I wanted to represent my body, what sort of style did I want to exhibit; would I be a pious Christian or one of those slightly wild, but still safe kinds of people? You get the idea, I’m sure you’ve been there before.
   But there was a more intense, pressing matter that continually weighed on my brain. What was I going to call myself? This was my chance to remake myself if I so chose. I suppose you must also understand that I introduce myself by my middle name, forever hiding my first name. During my childhood, first names were sort of a family secret, but that’s for another time.
   I repeatedly found myself caught between the studious and attentive ‘Melissa’ during class (I liked to think she was the sophisticated type), and the friendly and quite (uncharacteristically) out-going ‘Ashley’ among my peers. And so I can only conclude that at this very point in time, sitting in this chair, I am a strange amalgamation of the two personalities. So hello, I would like to introduce myself. I am currently calling myself Melissa Ashley for the time being and I would like to cordially invite you to take a gander.
